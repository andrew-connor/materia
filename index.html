Materia.

Object Composition Viewer

Materia shows everyday and iconic objects alongside what they are actually made of.

The left panel, Form, is the object as it exists. The right panel, Composition, breaks that same object into its constituent materials and renders each as a cube sized by volume. Both views share the same scale in meters, so you can compare the object directly to the size of the stuff inside it.

Controls
  • Choose object — Use the dropdown at the top-left of the toolbar to switch between the available objects.
  • Rotate — Left-click and drag inside either view (or one-finger drag on a trackpad). Both views rotate together unless unlocked.
  • Zoom — Scroll the mouse wheel, or pinch / two-finger scroll on a trackpad.
  • Pan — Right-click and drag for free pan in any direction, or use the ↑ / ↓ buttons in the lower-left of each view to pan vertically.
  • Lock — The lock toggle in the toolbar keeps both views synchronized. Turn it off to inspect each view independently.
  • Units — Switch volume readouts between m³ and ft³ in the toolbar.
  • Measurements — Toggle the X / Y / Z dimension lines on both views with the Measurements button. They're on by default.
  • Reset View — Re-frames both panels on the current object.
Volume figures are illustrative — directionally correct, but approximate.
